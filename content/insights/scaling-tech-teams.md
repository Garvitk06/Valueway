# Sourcing AI Talents

With the AI gold rush showing no signs of slowing down, the demand for specialized machine learning scientists, research engineers, and LLM architects has reached historic heights. Recruiting for these roles requires technical literacy and customized sourcing networks.

## Identifying Real ML Capabilities

Because "AI" has become a marketing term, technical recruiters must separate generic developers from true scientists. Evaluate:

*   Contributions to open-source libraries (PyTorch, JAX, Hugging Face)
*   Published peer-reviewed literature (NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR)
*   Deep comprehension of distributed training cluster setups (CUDA, NCCL, InfiniBand)

## Navigating Talent Scarcity

Since the pool of top-tier AI researchers is small, standard passive postings are ineffective. Planners should:

*   Leverage professional developer networking portals (GitHub, Hugging Face)
*   Offer highly customizable, remote-first contract options
*   Build relations with university research departments for graduate sourcing

## Structuring Technical Audits

The interviewing loop must focus on practical competency. Designing structured code challenges, live system-design sessions, and paper review discussions guarantees matching with engineers who can immediately configure model training runs.
